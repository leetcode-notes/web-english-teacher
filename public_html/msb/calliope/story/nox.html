Nox

According the Greek story of creation, at first there was nothing but Chaos. Chaos was a huge, wild, disorganized void or "nothingness." Chaos had two children: Nyx and Erebus. They were not gods to be worshipped. They were personifications of concepts that storytellers used.

Nyx laid an egg, and from it came love. When Love was born, beauty and order came, too. The confusion of Chaos began to disappear. Love created Light and Day.

In some stories, Nyx rode across the sky in a chariot, bringing night.

The Roman name for Nyx was Nox, and our English words come from Nox.

This story is derived from information in Mythology by Edith Hamilton, published by Little, Brown, and Co. of Boston, 1942.
