Mark Twain and Laughter: a WebQuest

 

Process

Introduction Task Process Resources Evaluation Conclusion For Teachers Standards Credits

First

You will need to know a little bit about the award itself, so read about the award and its past recipients first. Write a summary of this information.

Second

Research and analyze what makes things funny. Read about different elements of humor. (See list on the Resources page, too. Make a list of the types of humor you believe are common today. Include examples that are appropriate for school. (Skip "blue humor.")

Third

You need to learn about Mark Twain's style of humor. To do that, complete the following tasks:

  • Read the excerpt from Twain's autobiography, Life on the Mississippi, found in your textbook beginning on p. 453. What type(s) of humor are most common in that excerpt? Write a paragraph about the humor in this excerpt and be sure to quote examples.
  • Read the Twain one-liners on p. 465. What commonalities do you notice about them? How are they different from one-liners by, say, Dave Chappelle, Margaret Cho, or Larry the Cable Guy? Take notes as you discuss them - you may want this information when you write the contents of your brochure. You may find it helpful to refer to the list you made as your second task.
  • Read at least 2 more short stories by Mark Twain. One of them MUST be "The Celebrated Jumping Frog of Calaveras County." You may choose the second story yourself. Hand in one-paragraph summaries of the two stories.
  • Make a list of the types of humor that you notice most often in Twain's short stories. Which of those types, if any, is also common today? Add these observations to your list of notes from your discussion of the one-liners.
  • Watch the video Mark Twain Tonight starring Hal Holbrook. How is your understanding of his humor affected by seeing it performed instead of reading it? Write a response to the video.

Fourth

By the time you have completed all of the above, you have gathered a great deal of information about Mark Twain. You are ready to write and design your brochure. Click on "Resources" for help with brochure design and other links.
