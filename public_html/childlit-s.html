The Gingerbread Man by Karen Schmidt
Four activities: comparing Schmidt's story with Jan Brett's The Gingerbread Baby; baking, decorating, and describing gingerbread cookies, and choosing a favorite cookie. These ideas can involve families. Adobe Reader required for access.

Using 90th Street's Advice
First, writers will search for all four pieces of advice that young author, Eva, receives from her neighbors and then uses in her story found in the book Nothing Ever Happens on 90th Street by Roni Schotter. Next, writers will apply the four pieces of advice as they brainstorm details about a person, place, and thing they have chosen to write about. Finally, writers will each create a descriptive paragraph that interestingly describes the person, place, and thing they have chosen.
