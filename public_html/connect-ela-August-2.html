Pages with New Links

  • Bulletin Boards
    Love them or hate them, bulletin boards are an essential part of our classroom ambiance. Here are some ideas on what to do with them.
  • Dear Ray Bradbury:
    A middle school student writes about the impact reading Fahrenheit 451 had on her. Short writing prompt: write a comment for the bottom of the blog, responding to the student. Longer writing prompt: at what points do you agree and why? Where do you disagree and why?
  • Homer's The Odyssey
    Journal prompts, vocabulary, study guide questions, learning activities: an epic set of teaching resources!
  • Helen Lester
    Lesson plans for Author, a True Story.
  • Nouns
    What's the difference between a concrete noun and an abstract noun? Go on a scavenger hunt to find out! And other ideas for teaching nouns.
  • Prepositions
    Two new lessons here, one using MacLachlan's All the Places to Love as a mentor text, and one focusing on weather. Both teach sentence structure in the contexts of reading and writing.
  • Punctuation
    An article on nonexistent but needed punctuation marks suggests a writing prompt.
  • Research
    Introduce emerging writers to the research process by having them produce trading cards about famous objects or landmarks.
  • David Shannon
    Extend A Bad Case of Stripes: what else could go wrong and how might it be resolved?
  • Vocabulary
    Among other suggestions here, find a way to build vocabulary using sticky notes.
Special mention:
  • ySight Words
    This new site supports emerging readers and their parents.
  • PBS Learning Media
    PBS has long been a good friend to educators. Check out their amazing resources and support PBS as you can!
  • 25 Alternatives to Using the Word "Great"
    A teacher reflects on an overused word and offers other choices.
  • "I don't think teachers know what they're doing"
    Words of appreciation from a parent.
