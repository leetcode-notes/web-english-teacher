Keely's Mountain
In this blended lesson supporting literacy skills, students are introduced to Keely, who feels that her identity as a Nipmuc Native American is weakened because there is no strong Nipmuc community around her. The video Keely made when she was young and felt discouraged is countered by her positive thoughts about identity when she is older. Students develop their literacy skills through an English language arts focus on symbolism. During this process, they read informational text, learn and practice vocabulary words, and explore content through videos and interactive activities. Designed for grades 5-8.

Nothing to Fear by Jackie French Koller. Adobe Reader required for these resources.

  • Introductory Material
    Background information, character list, vocabulary words.
  • Author's Note-Chapter 12 Worksheet
    Students chart details that help them see the effects of the Great Depression. They work with analogies and make predictions.
  • Chapters 13-28 Worksheet
    Students monitor their reading, work with synonyms, and make predictions.
  • Chapters 29-48 Worksheet
    Students make inferences, work with connotations, and think beyond the novel.
  • Biographical and Historical Approaches to Literary Works
    Students identify vivid details.
  • Historical Setting Graphic Organizer
    Students note note ways that the historical setting influences events, actions and feelings.
  • Style: Diction and Sentence Structure
    Students note words, phrases, and sentences that represent the characters' ways of talking. Then they describe the style and effect of each example.
  • Whole-Novel Discussion and Activities
    4 discussion questions and 2 post-reading activities.
  • Multiple Choice Test
    10 questions.
