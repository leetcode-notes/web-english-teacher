The Best Illustrations from 150 Years of Alice in Wonderland
Writing prompts:

  • Aside from topic, how are these illustrations similar? How are they different? Include references to the book.
  • Choose one of Lisbeth Zwerger's illustrations. Explain how her use of distortion complements the text.
  • The drawings by Robert Steadman are black-and-white only. What advantage do you see to that style, if any? What disadvantage, if any? Include references to the book.
  • The illustrations by Tove Jansson and John Vernon Lord have color in some and not in others. What difference does color make in the illustrations? Include key quotations where possible.
  • The drawings by Leonard Weisgard begin simply and become increasingly complex and fanciful. In what ways does this complement the text? Refer to specific passages.
  • Salvador Dali often "colors outside the line." What does this add to his illustrations? Include key quotations where possible.
  • Circles and other geometric shapes dominate the illustrations by Yayoi Kusama. How does this contribute to her illustrations? Include reference to specific passages where possible.
  • The last set of pictures is from a pop-up book by Robert Sabuda. What does the 3-D art offer than 2-D doesn't, in terms of Alice in Wonderland? Include reference to the text where possible.
