Unique Metaphor Collections
Inspired by the first chapter of the novel Girl with a Pearl Earring by Tracy Chevalier, students will discover metaphors in their world and reading and create their own unique metaphorical descriptions for a collection they devote a page to in their writers notebooks or their journals. Once a collection of unique metaphors has been built in the writers notebook, teachers can challenge students to create a longer piece of writing (poem, narrative, reflection, etc.) that is inspired by one or several of their metaphors.

Roughnecks by Paul Cochran. Adobe Reader required for these resources.

  • Introductory Material
    Background information, character list, vocabulary words.
  • Chapters 1- 6 Worksheet
    Students practice making generalizations. They work with synonyms and antonyms, and they make predictions.
  • Chapters 7- 11 Worksheet
    Students make inferences about character motivation. They work with synonyms and connotations, and they make predictions.
  • Chapters 12-18 Worksheet
    Students practice drawing conclusions. They work with figurative language and think beyond the novel.
  • Ambiguity Graphic Organizer
    Students consider interpretations and the purpose of the ambiguous ending.
  • First-Person Point of View Graphic Organizer
    Students analyze the main character.
  • Setting and Mood Graphic Organizer
    Students analyze the relationship between setting and mood.
  • Whole-Novel Discussion and Activities
    6 discussion questions and 2 post-reading activities.
  • Multiple Choice Test
    10 questions.
